About the Project

A Space That Heals, Gathers & Grows

Montefiore Square sits at the intersection of Broadway and 138th Street in Harlem — a neighborhood rich in culture, history, and community spirit. Yet today the plaza falls short of its potential: hard surfaces, minimal greenery, and a lack of comfortable seating leave residents with little reason to linger.


This project proposes a comfort and wellness redesign — introducing shade trees, flexible seating, soft landscaping, and programmed spaces that invite residents to rest, gather, and thrive.


Essential Question: How can urban plaza design promote physical comfort, mental wellness, and community belonging in a dense city neighborhood?

The Problem

What's Wrong With the Plaza Today?

Montefiore Square currently fails to meet the basic comfort and wellness needs of Harlem residents. The space is dominated by hard pavement, offers almost no shade on hot days, and lacks the amenities that encourage people to spend time outdoors.


For a neighborhood where many residents live in crowded apartments with limited private outdoor space, a welcoming public plaza is not a luxury — it is essential infrastructure for community health.

🌡️

Heat & No Shade

Hard pavement absorbs heat. No tree canopy means no relief during summer months.

🪑

Lack of Seating

Minimal benches and no comfortable, varied seating for different users.

🌿

No Greenery

Absence of plants or nature makes the space feel harsh and uninviting.

👥

No Programming

Nothing draws people to gather — no activities or community-oriented spaces.

Site & Context

Understanding Montefiore Square

Located in Central Harlem at Broadway and W 138th Street, Montefiore Square is surrounded by residential buildings, transit stops, and community institutions. Its users are diverse — seniors, families, commuters, and youth — each with different needs a wellness redesign must address.

01

Location

Broadway & W 138th St, Central Harlem — high foot traffic from nearby subway and bus stops.

02

Primary Users

Seniors seeking rest, families with children, daily commuters, and local residents needing outdoor space.

03

Key Challenge

High-density neighborhood with limited access to green, comfortable public outdoor space.

Design Options

Three Directions Explored

Using DALL·E, three distinct redesign concepts were generated and compared before selecting the strongest direction for the final proposal.

Option 01

Green Canopy Plaza

Dense tree planting and overhead shade structures create a cool, forested refuge in the city.

Option 02

Wellness Garden

Seasonal plantings, a small water feature, and flexible seating zones for rest and mindfulness.

Option 03

Community Gathering Hub

Open lawn areas, a small amphitheater, and shaded clusters that support events and programming.

Final Proposal

The Wellness Garden Plaza

The selected proposal transforms Montefiore Square into a layered wellness garden — combining shade, nature, seating, and community space into one cohesive design.

Design Justification

Why This Design?

The Wellness Garden Plaza was selected because it directly addresses the three core problems: heat, lack of greenery, and no reason to stay. It balances the needs of multiple user groups — seniors wanting quiet rest spots, families needing active space — while fitting within realistic NYC DOT plaza constraints.


The design is inspired by successful NYC plaza transformations like Westchester Square and Myrtle/Wyckoff Plaza, adapted to the specific culture and density of Central Harlem.

🌳

Canopy Trees & Shade

Native street trees reduce surface heat by up to 10°F and create a natural ceiling over the plaza.

🪑

Varied Seating Zones

Movable chairs, fixed benches, and low garden walls give users choice in how they sit and gather.

🌸

Seasonal Planting Beds

Native perennials that change with the seasons, bringing color, life, and biodiversity year-round.

💧

Water Feature

A small fountain provides ambient sound, cools the air, and becomes a focal gathering point.

Reflection & Conclusion

Human Judgment Meets AI Exploration

This project used AI image generation as a design exploration tool — not a replacement for design thinking. Here is an honest reflection on what AI helped with and where human judgment was essential.

What AI Helped With

  • Rapidly generating multiple visual directions from text prompts
  • Visualizing abstract ideas like "green wellness plaza" as concrete images
  • Producing before/after comparisons quickly and easily
  • Exploring color palettes, materials, and planting styles
  • Iterating on designs without needing manual drawing skills

What Required Human Judgment

  • Understanding the real needs of Harlem community members
  • Selecting which design direction was most appropriate and realistic
  • Evaluating images for cultural sensitivity and local context
  • Deciding which features were feasible for a NYC DOT plaza
  • Writing the narrative, justification, and academic framing

"AI was a powerful brainstorming partner — but every meaningful design decision came from understanding the people and the place, not from a prompt."
